Andhra Pradesh - The Land of Rich Culture and Heritage

Discover the captivating beauty, heritage, and cultural richness of Andhra Pradesh, a state known for its diverse landscapes, historic temples, and vibrant traditions.

Overview

Andhra Pradesh, located on the southeastern coast of India, is bordered by Telangana, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Karnataka, and Tamil Nadu. The state is known for its historic temples, scenic landscapes, and a rich cultural heritage rooted in both Hinduism and Buddhism.

Capital City - Amaravati

Amaravati, the capital of Andhra Pradesh, is situated on the banks of the Krishna River. It is an ancient city with significant archaeological sites, including Buddhist stupas and relics, showcasing its historical importance as a spiritual hub.

Language

Telugu, one of India's classical languages, is the official language of Andhra Pradesh. Known for its rich literary tradition, Telugu literature includes renowned poetry, dramas, and epics that are still celebrated today. English and Urdu are also spoken in certain regions.

Culture

Andhra Pradesh has a diverse culture with deep-rooted traditions. The state is famous for its classical dance form, Kuchipudi, which combines graceful movements and storytelling. Festivals like Ugadi (New Year), Vijayadashami, and Makara Sankranti are celebrated with much enthusiasm. The state is also known for its unique crafts like Kalamkari art, Pochampally silk weaving, and leather puppetry.
